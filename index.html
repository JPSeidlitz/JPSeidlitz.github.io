    Robotics

    During my time as part of the RoboVikes, I learned not only how to program proficiently, but also how to communicate complex ideas clearly and concisely. I developed my skills as a programmer, not only learning how to perform actions, but also how to write code that other programmers can read and use easily. This skill was invaluable when I became the lead programmer for the team as I had to constantly share my code and work collaboratively with my crew. In the 2024 season my leadership was recognized as I was nominated by my team for the Dean’s List award, an award given to an individual student for leadership, and placed as a semi-finalist at the Fort Worth District Event.

    Theater

    While robotics taught me complex ideas and clear communication, technical theater taught me visual design, organizational thinking, and professional communication. I drafted and workshopped lighting plots and reached out to professionals for both feedback and to rent them for our performances. Once the lights were in the theater, I led my crew in designing cues, color schemes, and effects for the show. During shows, I was the liaison between the director and the light and sound crew heads going through the cues.
